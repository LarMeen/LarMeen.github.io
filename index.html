Discover Belarus

Belarus, officially known as the Republic of Belarus, is a country in Eastern Europe. It gained its independence following the dissolution of the Soviet Union in 1991. The official languages are Belarusian and Russian, reflecting its historical ties and cultural heritage.

Head of State

The head of state in Belarus is the President. The role of the President includes overseeing foreign policy, national security, and serving as the commander-in-chief of the armed forces.

Head of Government

The head of government in Belarus is the Prime Minister. The Prime Minister is responsible for overseeing the country's executive branch and managing domestic policy. The role involves coordinating the activities of the ministries and implementing economic and social policies.

Population

Belarus has a population of approximately 9.3 million people. The population is concentrated mainly in urban areas, with Minsk, the capital, being the largest city.

Capital

The capital of Belarus is Minsk. It is the largest city in the country and serves as the political, economic, and cultural center.

Regional Cities

Belarus is home to several important regional cities, each contributing to the country's cultural and economic landscape.

Monetary Unit

The monetary unit of Belarus is the Belarusian ruble (BYN). It is used for all financial transactions within the country and is managed by the National Bank of the Republic of Belarus.
